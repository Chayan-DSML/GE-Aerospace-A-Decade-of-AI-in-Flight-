The Power of Digital Twins & Supercomputing

GE Aerospace is a top industrial user of U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) exascale supercomputers, enabling engineers to run highly complex simulations of engine performance. This computational power is crucial for training the AI models that optimize designs for factors like aerodynamics and noise reduction. The Open Fan architecture, for instance, is being refined through these simulations, which model its integration with the aircraft wing to maximize efficiency. This symbiotic relationship between supercomputing and AI accelerates breakthroughs, making ambitious programs like RISE possible.

Optimizing Logistics & Pilot Readiness

This area highlights AI's critical role in transforming aircraft maintenance from a reactive to a predictive model. By analyzing data from a global fleet of over 70,000 engines, GE's AI systems, including Digital Twins and the Blade Inspection Tool (BIT), forecast maintenance needs with remarkable accuracy. This ensures higher aircraft availability, enhances safety, and significantly reduces operational costs for both commercial and military customers.
